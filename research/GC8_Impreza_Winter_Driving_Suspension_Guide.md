## ⚙️ Suspension Setup for Winter Performance

### Factory Suspension Characteristics (GC8)
| Component | Stock Spec | Winter Notes |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Spring rate | ~3.5 kg/mm front, ~2.8 kg/mm rear | Soft enough for snow compliance |
| Ride height | ~140mm ground clearance | Adequate for most snow conditions |
| Damping | Conservative | Body roll in spirited driving |
| Sway bars | Moderate | Understeer-biased for safety |

### Recommended Winter Setup Philosophy

**Option 1: Stock+ (Best for Daily + Spirited)**
- **Keep stock springs & ride height** — ground clearance matters in deep snow
- **Upgrade dampers:** Bilstein B6/B8 or KYB Excel-G
  - Better body control without sacrificing compliance
  - Handles spirited driving + rough winter roads
- **Rear sway bar upgrade:** 22mm → 24mm
  - Reduces understeer (factory AWD tuning is nose-heavy)
  - Makes car rotate more willingly
  - Adjustable endlinks for fine-tuning
- **Cost:** $600-1200 installed

**Option 2: Mild Coilovers (Enthusiast)**
- **BC Racing BR series** or **Fortune Auto 500**
  - Height adjustable (raise for winter, lower for summer)
  - Damping adjustable (soft for snow, stiff for summer autocross)
- **Set ride height:** +10mm over stock for winter
  - Protects underbody from snowbanks
  - Maintains decent handling
- **Damping:** 50-60% soft for snow compliance
- **Cost:** $1000-1500 + install

**Option 3: Full Rally Prep (Hardcore)**
- **Rally-spec coilovers:** Ohlins, DG1, or custom valving
- **Increased travel:** +20-30mm over stock
- **Skid plates:** Protect oil pan, transmission
- **Cost:** $3000-5000+

### Alignment for Winter Fun

**Street Setup (Daily + Spirited):**
| Setting | Front | Rear | Why |
|---------|-------|------|-----|
| Camber | -1.5° to -2.0° | -1.0° to -1.5° | Balanced tire wear, good turn-in |
| Toe | 0° to +1/16" | +1/8" total | Stable at speed, slight rotation bias |
| Caster | +5° to +6° | N/A | Self-centering, high-speed stability |

**Slide-Happy Setup (Snow Lot Fun):**
| Setting | Front | Rear | Why |
|---------|-------|------|-----|
| Camber | -2.0° to -2.5° | -1.5° to -2.0° | Aggressive turn-in |
| Toe | 0° | +1/4" total | Rear wants to rotate more |
| Caster | +6° to +7° | N/A | Sharp steering response |

**Tips:**
- More rear toe-in = more oversteer (easier to slide)
- More negative camber = better grip mid-corner
- Get aligned at fall (before winter) and spring (after winter)

## 🏔️ Tire Strategy for Alaska Winter

### Tire Hierarchy (Performance in Snow)

**Tier 1: Dedicated Winter Tires (Best)**
1. **Nokian Hakkapeliitta R5** — studless winter king
   - Best snow/ice grip without studs
   - Expensive but worth it ($800-1000/set)
2. **Bridgestone Blizzak WS90** — excellent all-around
   - Quieter than Nokian on dry pavement
   - Slightly less ice grip, better value (~$600-800/set)
3. **Michelin X-Ice Snow** — long-lasting
   - 40k mile tread life (vs. 25k for Blizzaks)
   - Good balance of snow/dry performance

**Tier 2: Studded Tires (If Legal in Alaska)**
- **Nokian Hakkapeliitta 10** — studded ice domination
  - Best ice grip, period
  - Loud on dry pavement
  - Tears up roads (check local regulations)

**Tier 3: All-Season Performance (Acceptable)**
- **Michelin CrossClimate2** — "winter-rated all-season"
  - 3PMSF (Three Peak Mountain Snowflake) rated
  - Good for light snow, terrible on ice
  - Alaska? Nah.

**Sizing for GC8 Impreza:**
- Stock: 185/70R14 or 195/60R15
- **Winter recommendation:** Same width or narrower
  - Narrower = better snow penetration
  - 175/70R14 or 185/65R15 work great

### Tire Pressure for Snow

**On-Road (Plowed Streets):**
- Front: 32-34 PSI
- Rear: 30-32 PSI
- Slight stagger = better rotation

**Snow Lot / Deep Snow:**
- Front: 28-30 PSI
- Rear: 26-28 PSI
- Lower pressure = better flotation, more contact patch

**DO NOT go below 25 PSI** — risk of bead unseating or tire damage

## 🎿 Spirited Winter Driving Techniques

### The AWD Advantage (& Its Limits)

**What Subaru AWD Does:**
- Distributes power 60/40 front/rear (varies by model)
- Automatically sends power to wheels with grip
- **Helps you GO in snow**

**What Subaru AWD Doesn't Do:**
- Help you STOP (that's tires + brakes)
- Help you TURN (that's tires + physics)
- Defy the laws of friction

**Key Insight:** AWD = overconfidence trap. You can accelerate in conditions where you CAN'T stop or turn. Respect the limits.

### Controlled Slide Techniques

**1. Scandinavian Flick (Weight Transfer Initiation)**
- Turn AWAY from corner briefly (0.5s)
- Weight shifts to outside
- Turn IN sharply
- Throttle to break rear loose
- **Best for:** Initiating rotation on moderate-speed corners

**2. Lift-Off Oversteer (Engine Braking)**
- Enter corner with moderate speed
- Lift throttle mid-corner
- Weight transfers forward, rear lightens
- Rear starts to slide out
- **Catch with:** Slight throttle + countersteer
- **Best for:** Adjusting mid-corner rotation

**3. Handbrake Turn (Low-Speed Parking Lot)**
- Approach corner at 15-25 mph
- Clutch in + yank handbrake
- Rear locks, car rotates
- Countersteer + release handbrake
- Throttle to power out
- **Best for:** Snow lot fun, U-turns

**4. Power-On Oversteer (Throttle Control)**
- Enter corner, turn in
- Apply more throttle than grip allows
- Rear starts to slide (AWD will fight this more than RWD)
- Modulate throttle to control slide angle
- **Best for:** Constant-radius corners

### Recovery Techniques

**Understeer (Front Sliding):**
- **Wrong:** More steering input (makes it worse)
- **Right:** Lift throttle slightly, reduce steering angle
- Weight shifts forward → front grips → turn-in happens

**Oversteer (Rear Sliding):**
- **Countersteer** (steer into the slide)
- Modulate throttle (too much = spin, too little = snap back)
- Look where you want to go (not at the snowbank)

**Full Spin:**
- **Both feet in** (clutch + brake)
- Straighten wheels
- Come to a stop
- Assess, breathe, continue

## 🛡️ Safety & Legality

### Know Your Limits
1. **Empty parking lot practice FIRST**
   - Learn how YOUR car behaves
   - Find the grip limit in controlled environment
   - Practice recovery techniques
2. **Public roads = public responsibility**
   - Other drivers don't expect slides
   - Keep it on back roads with no traffic
   - Never risk others' safety for your fun
3. **Mechanical sympathy**
   - AWD system isn't designed for constant abuse
   - Clutch wear from handbrake turns
   - Tires wear faster from sliding

### Alaska-Specific Considerations
- **Studded tire season:** Oct 1 - Apr 15 (check current regs)
- **Black ice danger:** Common on bridges, shaded areas
- **Moose/wildlife:** More dangerous than snow
- **Cell service:** Spotty in rural areas (carry emergency kit)

### Emergency Kit (Trunk Essentials)
- [ ] Snow shovel (collapsible)
- [ ] Traction boards or kitty litter
- [ ] Ice scraper + brush
- [ ] Jumper cables
- [ ] Blanket
- [ ] Flashlight + batteries
- [ ] First aid kit
- [ ] Water + non-perishable snacks
- [ ] Spare winter gloves/hat

## 🔧 Winter Prep Checklist

### Before First Snow (October)
- [ ] **Install winter tires** (all 4 corners, no mixing)
- [ ] **Check battery** (cold kills weak batteries)
  - 12.6V+ resting = good
  - <12.4V = replace before winter
- [ ] **Test block heater** (if equipped)
- [ ] **Inspect wiper blades** (replace if streaking)
- [ ] **Top off washer fluid** (winter formula, -40°F rated)
- [ ] **Check coolant** (antifreeze ratio, should be -40°F minimum)
- [ ] **Inspect belts/hoses** (cold makes brittle rubber fail)

### Monthly Winter Checks
- [ ] **Tire pressure** (drops ~1 PSI per 10°F temperature drop)
- [ ] **Undercarriage wash** (salt removal, prevent rust)
- [ ] **Check for leaks** (head gasket weeping worsens in cold)

### After Winter (April)
- [ ] **Thorough undercarriage wash** (final salt removal)
- [ ] **Switch to summer tires** (if running dedicated winters)
- [ ] **Spring alignment** (winter potholes knock things out)
- [ ] **Inspect suspension** (look for damaged boots, leaking dampers)
